Safety & Procedures During Active Tours (Trekking, Horse Riding, etc.)

Nomads Voyage prioritizes your safety during all active tours, including trekking, horse riding, and other adventure activities. Our guides are trained in first aid and emergency protocols, but we also require your cooperation to ensure a smooth and secure experience.

  • Follow Guide Instructions: Always listen to your guide’s directions regarding terrain, pace, and safety measures.
  • Stay with the Group: Do not wander off alone. If you need to stop, inform the guide immediately.
  • Equipment Check: Ensure your gear (helmets, saddles, trekking poles) is properly fitted before starting.
  • Physical Limits: Communicate any fatigue, dizziness, or health concerns to the guide—do not push beyond your capacity.
  • Weather & Terrain Changes: Tours may adjust or cancel due to sudden weather (storms, landslides, extreme cold).
  • Emergency Situations: In case of injury, the guide will administer first aid and arrange evacuation if needed.
  • Nomads Voyage is not liable for risks taken against guide advice (e.g., ignoring safety warnings).
During Cultural & Wildlife Activities (Eagle Hunting Show, etc.)
  • Respect Animals: Keep a safe distance from eagles, horses, or other animals unless instructed otherwise.
  • No Sudden Movements: Avoid startling animals during demonstrations (e.g., eagle shows).
  • Photography Rules: Follow guidelines on flash usage and proximity to wildlife.
  • Children Supervision: Parents must monitor children closely around animals.
General Responsibility
  • Insurance: Ensure you have travel insurance covering adventure activities.
  • Alcohol/Drugs: Strictly prohibited during active tours for safety reasons.
  • Personal Belongings: Nomads Voyage is not responsible for lost/damaged items during activities.
  • Important: Your guide’s instructions are mandatory for your safety. If you ignore their directions, you assume full responsibility for any consequences, including injuries, accidents, or additional costs (e.g., emergency evacuations).
